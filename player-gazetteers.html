1. Gazetteers

1.1. PC Facing

The following represents the world as you have heretofore expereinced it.

1.1.1. Setting Primer

The following is what most folk know — or think they know — about the world. It is not complete. It is not always accurate. But it is a place to start.

  1. Everyday Life
    1. The World as It Is

      Before anything else, understand this: The world is not made for heroes. It is made for people trying to get by.

    2. Daily Life

      Most people:

      • Are born in one place
      • Live there all their lives
      • Work from sunrise to sunset

      They farm, build, trade, or serve. They worry about:

      • Food
      • Weather
      • Illness
      • Making it through the next season

      Life is not easy—but it is familiar.

    3. Towns and Villages

      Most settlements are small. They are:

      • Built from wood, stone, and whatever is at hand
      • Crowded in places, quiet in others
      • Full of smoke, noise, and life

      People live close together. They know each other's business. And strangers are noticed.

    4. Work and Survival

      Nothing is wasted. People:

      • Mend what breaks
      • Share when they must
      • Save what they can

      A good harvest matters. A bad one can ruin everything. Coin is important—but not always easy to come by.

    5. Travel

      Most people do not travel far. The road is:

      • Slow
      • Unpredictable
      • Sometimes dangerous

      Caravans move goods between places. Travellers bring news—and trouble. Leaving home is a serious decision.

    6. Law and Authority

      In some places, the law is strong. In others, it depends on who is in charge that day. Most people:

      • Keep their heads down
      • Avoid trouble where they can

      Justice can be:

      • Fair
      • Harsh
      • Or something in between
    7. The Land

      The land is not empty. Forests are:

      • Thick
      • Dark
      • Easy to get lost in

      Hills and valleys hide things. Some of those things are best left alone. People learn early: Stay where it is safe.

    8. Danger

      Danger exists—but not always in the open. It can be:

      • A bandit on the road
      • A sickness in the village
      • A strange happening no one can explain

      Most people:

      • Avoid risk
      • Trust slowly
      • And know when to walk away
    9. People

      People are:

      • Cautious
      • Practical
      • Often suspicious at first

      But also:

      • Capable of kindness
      • Loyal once trust is earned
      • Willing to help when it matters

      Communities survive because people rely on each other.

    10. Faith and Belief

      People believe in the gods. They:

      • Pray when they are afraid
      • Give thanks when things go well
      • Hope someone is listening
      • And hope they're in a good mood

      Faith is part of life—not something separate from it.

    11. Final Word

      The world is hard. But it is not empty. There is:

      • Warmth in a shared meal
      • Comfort in familiar places
      • And hope, however small, in carrying on

      If you are careful, you may survive. If you are brave, you may do more. But nothing will come easily. And that is how things are.

  2. The Wider World
    1. What Folk Have Heard

      A rough account of distant lands, based on rumour, travellers' tales, and what little common folk tend to know. Most of this is second-hand. Some of it is wrong.

    2. The Empire

      A great land to the north and east. People say:

      • It is the largest and most powerful human realm
      • Full of cities, soldiers, and priests
      • Always dealing with war, unrest, or worse

      You hear that:

      • The roads are better than here
      • Law is stricter, but more reliable
      • There is always work… if you can find it

      Some say it is the safest place in the world. Others say it only looks that way from a distance.

    3. Bretonnia

      A land of knights and nobles, across the mountains. People say:

      • Knights ride in shining armour and follow strict codes
      • Honour means everything to them
      • Peasants work the land and know their place

      You hear that:

      • A knight's word is his bond
      • The land is rich and fertile

      But also:

      • Life for common folk can be hard
      • Justice depends on who is judging
    4. Kislev

      A cold land far to the north. People say:

      • The people there are tough and stubborn
      • Winter lasts most of the year
      • They fight constantly against things from the north

      You hear that:

      • They ride strange horses and fight from the saddle
      • Their cities are built to withstand siege

      Most agree: If Kislev falls, the south will feel it soon enough.

    5. The Badlands

      To the south. No one sensible goes there. People say:

      • Orcs and goblins roam in great numbers
      • Nothing grows properly
      • Everything there wants to kill you

      Travellers claim:

      • Ruins lie scattered across the land
      • Some still hold treasure… or worse

      Most advice is simple: Stay away.

    6. The Mountains

      The great mountain ranges stretch across the world. People say:

      • Dwarfs live there in vast stone halls
      • The peaks are rich in ore and danger

      You hear that:

      • Passes are narrow and easily blocked
      • Many never come back from crossing them
    7. The Elven Lands

      Far away, and rarely seen. People say:

      • Elves have their own kingdoms, hidden from the world
      • They are ancient, powerful, and strange

      Some claim:

      • There is an island kingdom far across the sea
      • Forests where elves walk unseen among the trees

      Most folk will never see such places.

    8. The Distant South

      Beyond the Badlands and deserts. People say:

      • There are lands of great heat and strange peoples
      • Cities older than anything in the north

      Stories mention:

      • Jungles filled with ruins
      • Creatures no one can name

      Few who go there return with clear stories.

    9. The Far East

      So far away it might as well be another world. People say:

      • Great empires exist there
      • Their people are disciplined and strange in their ways

      But most of this is guesswork.

    10. The Seas

      Vast and dangerous. People say:

      • Storms can rise without warning
      • Sea monsters drag ships beneath the waves

      Sailors speak of:

      • Strange lights on the water
      • Things moving beneath the surface

      Best leave the sea to those who must travel it.

    11. The North

      No one speaks of it lightly. People say:

      • The land itself is cursed
      • Men who go there do not return… not as men

      You hear:

      • Warbands sometimes come south
      • Nothing good follows them

      It is enough to know: The north is where things go wrong.

    12. Final Word

      The world is wide. Wider than most will ever see. And full of places where:

      • Fortune might be found
      • Or where a person might vanish without a trace

      If you leave home, you will learn quickly: Not all stories are lies. And not all truths are safe to know.

  3. The Peoples of the World

    You will meet many kinds of people in your travels. Some are familiar. Others are not. What follows is what most folk know—or think they know.

    1. Humans

      That's you. Humans are found everywhere:

      • In the Empire
      • In the Border Princes
      • In distant lands

      People say:

      • Humans are adaptable
      • Quick to build, quick to fight
      • Not always quick to trust

      Most folk deal with their own kind first.

    2. Dwarfs

      Short, broad, and strong. Dwarfs are known for:

      • Craftsmanship
      • Stonework
      • Long memories

      People say:

      • They hold grudges for a very long time
      • Their word, once given, is solid
      • They prefer their own halls to the open world

      They are respected—but not always easy to deal with.

    3. Elves

      Tall, long-lived, and hard to read. Most people rarely see them. People say:

      • They are graceful and clever
      • They speak strangely and think differently
      • They do not forget anything

      Some claim there are different kinds of elves:

      • Those who live in forests
      • Those who come from across the sea

      Most folk don't know the difference. And most elves don't bother to explain.

    4. Halflings

      Small folk, often found living among humans. They are said to have their own homeland—a quiet, well-fed corner of the Empire known as the Moot, where they govern themselves in their own way. Whether it is as peaceful as they claim is anyone's guess.

      They are known for:

      • Cooking
      • Quick hands
      • Friendly manners

      People say:

      • They are easy to underestimate
      • They are rarely where trouble starts

      But sometimes:

      • They know more than they let on
    5. Greenskins

      Creatures of war. Orcs and goblins are:

      • Violent
      • Unpredictable
      • Always looking for a fight

      People say:

      • They gather in large numbers
      • They fight each other as often as anyone else
      • When they unite, it's bad for everyone

      If they're nearby, you'll hear about it quickly.

    6. Beastmen

      Things of the wild places. Half-man, half-beast—and something worse underneath. People say:

      • They come out of the deep forests
      • They hate what men have built
      • They strike without warning and leave little behind

      Most avoid deep forests for a reason. Whatever they once were, they are not that now.

    7. Ratmen

      Some say there are creatures like rats, but walking like men. Stories claim:

      • They live underground
      • They steal, spy, and spread disease

      Others say:

      • This is nonsense
      • Just stories to scare children

      Most people:

      • Laugh it off
      • Or change the subject

      Still… Strange things happen in dark places.

    8. Others

      There are said to be many more:

      • Creatures in distant lands
      • Beings that do not belong in the world at all

      Most folk never see them. And most are glad of it.

    9. Final Word

      The world is wider than you think. And not everything in it is meant to be understood.

      It is best to remember: Not everything that looks human is friendly. And not everything that isn't human is your enemy.

1.1.2. Regional Gazetteers

  1. The Border Princes

    This is where you were born. This is what you know. The Border Princes are not a single land, not really. They are a patchwork of:

    • Small territories
    • Crumbling forts
    • Villages clinging to life
    • Ruins from older times

    No one rules it all. If someone claims they do, they won't for long.

    1. What Folk Say

      People say:

      • Anyone can become a ruler here… if they can hold what they take
      • Law depends on who has the most soldiers nearby
      • Yesterday's lord can be tomorrow's corpse

      You grow up knowing:

      • Power is personal
      • Promises are only as good as the swords behind them
      • Trouble is never far away
    2. Settlements and Life

      Most places are:

      • Fortified villages
      • Rough towns built around trade routes
      • Old keeps taken over and rebuilt again and again

      Nothing here feels permanent. Even the oldest buildings often sit on:

      • Older foundations
      • Forgotten cellars
      • Buried ruins

      People don't always ask what lies beneath.

    3. Power

      From what you've seen, power usually belongs to:

      • Local rulers (often self-declared "princes")
      • Mercenary captains
      • Wealthy landowners
      • Anyone who can gather enough armed followers

      Alliances shift. Borders change. Sometimes overnight.

    4. Trade and Survival

      Trade keeps the Border Princes alive. Caravans bring:

      • Food
      • Tools
      • Weapons
      • News

      Without them, many places would not last a season. Merchants are:

      • Necessary
      • Watched carefully
      • Sometimes richer than the people they deal with

      Escorting a caravan is dangerous—but common work.

    5. Dangers

      You don't need to travel far to find trouble. Common threats include:

      • Bandits
      • Orcs and goblins
      • Beastmen from the wild places
      • Raiders from beyond the hills

      And then there are:

      • Old ruins no one fully explores
      • Places people avoid without saying why
    6. The Land

      The land is uneven and often unforgiving. You know:

      • Roads can vanish into mud after rain
      • Forests are thick and easy to get lost in
      • Hills and valleys hide things you don't want to find

      You learn early: Stay on known paths when you can.

    7. Opportunities

      For those willing to take risks, the Border Princes offer:

      • Work as a caravan guard or hired sword
      • Land—if you can take it and hold it
      • The chance to become something, for better or worse

      Nothing is given here. It is taken, held, or lost.

    8. Why People Stay or Leave

      Some stay because:

      • It is home
      • They have nowhere else to go

      Others leave because:

      • There is nothing left for them
      • Or because they believe they can do better elsewhere

      Many who leave do not return.

    9. Final Word

      The Border Princes are not stable. They are not safe. But they are yours. And if a person is willing to take risks— they can become something here that they never could anywhere else.

  2. The Empire

    The Empire is the greatest human realm in the known world. It lies to the north of the Border Princes, beyond the mountains. It is vast, old, and never quite at peace.

    To those who have only heard of it:

    • It is the standard against which other lands are measured
    • It is proof that humans can build something that lasts
    • It is a place where things are—supposedly—more orderly

    Whether that is true depends on where you stand.

    1. What People Say

      People say:

      • The Empire has armies that could swallow entire nations
      • Its cities are larger than anything in the Border Princes
      • Its roads go everywhere and carry everything

      You hear:

      • The law there is real law—not just the word of whoever holds the most swords
      • A person can make something of themselves there, if they work for it
      • But the Empire is not as united as it looks from a distance

      Some say the provinces squabble like princes. Some say the church holds more power than the throne. Most agree: it is complicated.

    2. Daily Life

      Common folk in the Empire live much as they do elsewhere:

      • Working the land or a trade
      • Keeping faith with their gods
      • Staying out of trouble

      But the Empire has more of everything:

      • More people
      • More rules
      • More priests
      • More soldiers

      There are courts and councils, guilds and orders. For a commoner, most of this is background noise. What matters is: work, food, safety. And in the Empire, those things are—usually—more available.

    3. The Emperor and the Provinces

      The Empire is ruled by an Emperor, chosen by powerful nobles called Electors. People say:

      • He commands great armies and keeps the provinces together
      • His word carries across the land

      But common folk also know:

      • Each province has its own lord and its own ways
      • Some provinces are wealthy; others are poor or troubled
      • A person from one end of the Empire may feel almost foreign to another

      The Emperor holds it together. Or so everyone hopes.

    4. Faith and the Gods

      The people of the Empire worship many gods. Temples and priests are found in most towns—sometimes more than one.

      The most widely known:

      • Sigmar — the Empire's own god, a great warrior-king who became divine. His cult is the most powerful.
      • Ulric — god of winter, wolves, and battle. Older than Sigmar, and his followers know it.
      • Shallya — goddess of mercy and healing. Her dove-marked temples are where the sick and the desperate go. Hard to find a town without one.
      • Taal and Rhya — gods of the wild and of the earth. Widely worshipped in rural areas.
      • Morr — god of the dead and of dreams. His black-robed priests bury the dead and keep them where they belong.

      People attend services. Or at least, they are expected to. And in hard times, they pray to whoever seems most likely to help.

    5. Trade and Roads

      The Empire's roads are one of its great achievements. Caravans travel between cities constantly:

      • Carrying grain, cloth, ore, and finished goods
      • Bringing news from distant provinces
      • Making the Empire function

      Rivers are just as important:

      • The Reik and its tributaries carry as much trade as the roads
      • River towns grow fat on what passes through them

      Merchants are powerful. Guilds are powerful. And money, in the Empire, is always moving.

    6. Soldiers and War

      The Empire is always preparing for war—or already fighting one. People say:

      • Its armies are well-trained and numerous
      • It uses gunpowder weapons that can level walls
      • It fights threats from every direction at once

      Even in peaceful times:

      • Soldiers are never far away
      • Militia are drilled in the larger towns
      • The roads are patrolled

      This is what keeps the Empire standing. Or so its people believe.

    7. Dangers

      The Empire is safer than many places. But it is not safe.

      Threats include:

      • Bandits and outlaws in the wilder provinces
      • Beastmen in the forests—more numerous than people like to admit
      • Unrest between provinces and noble houses
      • Disease in the crowded cities

      And then there are things people do not talk about openly:

      • Strange cults, meeting in cellars
      • Corruption in places of authority
      • Disappearances that no one can explain

      The Empire has a special force—witch hunters—who pursue the worst of this. People are glad they exist. And afraid of them in equal measure.

    8. Opportunities

      For someone willing to travel north:

      • Work is easier to find than in the Border Princes
      • Cities offer trades, guilds, and institutions
      • There are armies to join, caravans to escort, and temples that employ people

      But:

      • Competition is fierce
      • Outsiders are watched
      • And the rules—of law, of guild, of church—are more present than back home

      The Empire rewards those who fit its systems. It is harder on those who don't.

    9. As Others See It

      From the Border Princes, the Empire seems:

      • Stronger
      • Safer
      • More stable

      But also:

      • Distant
      • Complicated
      • Not always as fair as it sounds from a distance

      Still, many believe: If you can make it there, you can make it anywhere.

    10. Final Word

      The Empire is not a promised land. It is a place where power is organised, not absent. Where the dangers are better hidden—and the opportunities are real, but earned.

      If you go there, go with open eyes. It is not the Border Princes. And it will not treat you as one of its own until you prove you are.
